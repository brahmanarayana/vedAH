6. [^2]  Then in the following bright fortnights (at the time) stated above (Sūtra I), the father, filling his joined hands with water and turning his face towards the moon, worships it


[^2]:  I am not sure about the meaning of prathamoddiṣṭa eva. I have translated according to the commentary, which has the following note: prathamoddiṣṭa eva prathamaṃ yaḥ kāla uddiṣṭaḥ kathitaḥ tasminn eva kāle tṛtīyāyām ity etat.The commentary then mentions a reading prathamodita eva, in which udita may either be derived from vad or from ud-i.
